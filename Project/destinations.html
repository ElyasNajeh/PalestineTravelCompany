Destinations

Jerusalem :
Photo :
Descirption :
Jerusalem is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Palestine, combining rich history with great religious significance. Visitors from around the world come to see Al-Aqsa Mosque, the Dome of the Rock, and the Church of the Holy Sepulchre. The Old Citys traditional markets, handmade crafts, and local foods add to its charm, making it a spiritual and cultural attraction all year round.
Facts :
  • Jerusalem is the holiest city in Palestine and home to Al-Aqsa Mosque, the first Qibla and the third holiest site in Islam.
  • It contains the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, one of the most important Christian sites in the world.
  • The Dome of the Rock was built in the 7th century and is one of the oldest and most beautiful Islamic landmarks.
  • The Old City is surrounded by historic walls with eight gates built during the Ottoman period.
Gaza :
Photo :
Descirption :
Gaza is a coastal city on the Mediterranean Sea and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It combines deep history with modern life, known for its beautiful beaches, traditional markets, and handmade crafts. It is a great destination for historical and seaside tourism, where visitors can explore ancient sites and enjoy the sea and local food.
Facts :
  • Gaza is believed to have been visited by many travelers throughout history.
  • It is home to Al-Sayyed Hashim Mosque, where the grandfather of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) is believed to be buried.
  • Its coastal location makes it a popular spot for marine tourism.
Ramallah :
Photo :
Descirption :
Ramallah is a lively city located in the heart of the West Bank and serves as the administrative and cultural center of Palestine. It is known for its modern atmosphere, restaurants, cafes, and diverse markets. The city is a great destination for cultural and urban tourism, attracting visitors with its art events, music festivals, and cultural institutions that reflect Palestinian creativity and spirit.
Facts :
  • Ramallah is a historic city that flourished during the Ottoman era
  • It has several old churches that date back centuries,
  • The city is a hub for Palestinian art and culture, hosting annual festivals and exhibitions.
Jericho :
Photo :
Descirption :
Jericho is one of the oldest cities in the world, located near the Jordan River and the Dead Sea. It has a warm climate and lush greenery filled with palm trees and farms. The city is a major tourist destination thanks to its historical and archaeological sites, such as Tell es-Sultan and Hisham Palace, as well as its proximity to the Dead Sea, which attracts visitors for relaxation and natural therapy.
Facts :
  • Jericho is considered the oldest continuously inhabited city in the world, dating back over 10,000 years.
  • Tell es-Sultan is one of its key archaeological sites, with remains from ancient civilizations.
  • Hisham Palace is a famous Umayyad landmark decorated with beautiful mosaics.
Nablus :
Photo :
Descirption :
Nablus is an ancient Palestinian city located between Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal in the northern West Bank. Known as the " Mountain of Fire " for its history of resistance, it is one of Palestine most beautiful cities, with its mountains, springs, and traditional soap factories. The city is famous for its old markets and its signature dessert, Nabulsi knafeh, making it a perfect destination that blends heritage, culture, and tradition.
Facts :
  • Nablus is one of the oldest cities in the region, with a history that goes back more than 4,000 years.
  • The city is well known for its traditional Nabulsi soap, produced for hundreds of years and knafah and considered a symbol of Palestinian heritage.
  • The Old City includes many ancient mosques and markets that reflect traditional Islamic architecture.
